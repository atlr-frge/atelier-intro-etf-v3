The condition

Finance drew the head. Creative drew the torso. Operations drew the legs. Nobody saw what came before.

Finance
Replit / custom scripts

Solving compliance and reporting. Nothing they build reaches anyone else. No shared output format, no shared memory.

Creative
Google AI Studio / Midjourney

Generating. No connection to what Finance approved or what Operations is automating. Different context, different outputs.

Operations
Microsoft Copilot

Automating. Doesn’t know what Finance built or what Creative generated. Every session starts from scratch.

“The AI doesn’t know what the AI is doing.”

Why this compounds

Shadow AI.
Lock‑in.
Amnesia at every session.

  • Shadow AI is back People who can’t use ChatGPT on corporate hardware are bringing personal laptops. The data governance situation is worse than BYOD was in 2012, and nobody has a policy for it yet.
  • Every tool starts from scratch Copilot doesn’t know what Replit built. The AI your marketing team uses doesn’t know what your product team shipped. No shared memory layer. Every agent starts with amnesia.
  • Speed creates lock‑in before strategy can catch up Teams that moved fast picked tools. Tools became workflows. Workflows became dependencies. By the time anyone asks “should we do this differently” — the answer is yes, but it’ll cost you.
  • The vendors are incentivized to keep it fragmented Microsoft wants you all‑in on Copilot. Google wants Workspace AI. Salesforce wants Einstein. None of them want you building something portable. The exquisite corpse is their business model.
The vocabulary
Exquisite Corpse
Fragmented AI deployment with no shared context between systems. What most organizations are running right now.
Amnesia Deployment
Any AI tool with no persistent memory or shared context. Starts from scratch every session. The default state of enterprise AI.
Shadow AI
Unsanctioned AI tool use inside an organization — personal accounts, personal devices. Worse than BYOD was in 2012.
Compounding System
A deployment where each interaction makes the system smarter, vs. one that resets to zero each session. What we build.

The approach

Map. Design. Deploy.

Three phases. Each one builds on the last. Month six is smarter than month one because everything the system has learned is retained — not thrown away at the end of each session.

Phase 01
Map the Corpse

Before we build anything, we figure out what you already have. Most organizations have never seen this picture. It’s uncomfortable. It’s also necessary.

  • Inventory every AI tool in use — sanctioned and shadow
  • Map what data goes in, what comes out, where it touches
  • Identify what’s compliant, what’s exposed, what’s invisible
  • Find where you’re paying for the same capability five times
Phase 02
Design the Joints

The failure mode in fragmented AI isn’t the tools. It’s the absence of anything connecting them. We build the connective tissue your organization is missing.

  • A shared knowledge backbone — structured, agent-readable, git-backed
  • A unified context model — how your organization’s knowledge is organized and accessed
  • Integration bridges — lightweight connectors via MCP servers or API layers
  • Governance — who can access what, what data leaves the building, what gets logged
Phase 03
Deploy Intentionally

We don’t sell AI transformation. We deliver working systems against specific, measurable problems. Start narrow, prove the results, then expand.

  • One high-value workflow — manual, repetitive, and knowledge-intensive
  • Build the system, prove the results, then expand
  • Each deployment feeds the knowledge backbone
  • Month six is smarter than month one — by design, not accident
